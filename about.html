Interpreting Chinese Names

“Low” (pronounced as in “how” or “now” or “cow”) or 刘 (pronounced liu2) is my family name, and is typically the first word of a Chinese name; and “Chin Chau” or 进超 is the first name representing 2 chinese characters. It is common to see chinese names with family names written last or first names concatenated into one word, but since I have to luxury to explain it here, I’d kept the original form. It is what I use daily anyway.
